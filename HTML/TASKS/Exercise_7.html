Pharmaceutics is the scientific discipline concerned with the process of creating the dosage form (such as a pill for oral administration or a powder for intravenous injection) of a therapeutic that is to be used by patients. It encompasses drug formulation and manufacturing.

Key Concepts

Pharmaceutical biotechnology is a relatively new and growing field in which the principles of biotechnology are applied to the development of drugs. A majority of therapeutic drugs in the current market are bioformulations, such as antibodies, nucleic acid products and vaccines. Such bioformulations are developed through several stages that include: understanding the principles underlying health and disease; the fundamental molecular mechanisms governing the function of related biomolecules; synthesis and purification of the molecules; determining the product shelf life, stability, toxicity and immunogenicity; drug delivery systems; patenting; and clinical trials.

Did You Know?

1. Ancient Beginnings: Pharmaceuticals have roots stretching back thousands of years. Ancient civilizations like the Egyptians and Chinese used natural remedies to treat illnesses.

2. The First Synthetic Drug: In 1869, chloral hydrate became the first synthetic drug. It was used as a sedative and paved the way for modern drug development.

3. Penicillin (A Game Changer): Discovered by Alexander Fleming in 1928, penicillin was the first true antibiotic. It revolutionized medicine by effectively treating bacterial infections.
